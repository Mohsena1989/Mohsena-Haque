Welcome to my website

Japanese Ambassador to Bangladesh ITO Naoki today inaugurated a training course at Bangladesh-Korea Technical Training Centre (BKTTC) for returning female migrant workers without the certainty of an income source, who were forced to come back to Bangladesh when the pandemic broke out last year. The course, titled "Training on Women's Economic Leadership", was inaugurated under a project of UN Women undertaken last year, titled "Women and Girls at the Centre of COVID-19 Prevention". The project created alternative income-generating sources for women and was supported by the Government of Japan, with Bangladesh Nari Sramik Kendra (BNSK) as the implementing partner. As part of the project, they have been producing masks and running a "Women Cafe" in BKTTC as an alternative livelihood.
